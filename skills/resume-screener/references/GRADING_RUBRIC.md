# Grading Rubric

## 10-Level Grading Scale

| Grade | Label | Score | Description |
|-------|-------|-------|-------------|
| C | Not Qualified | 1/10 | Fails to meet basic requirements. Major gaps in multiple critical dimensions. |
| B- | Partially Qualified | 2/10 | Meets some basic requirements but has significant gaps in key areas. |
| B | Meets Basics | 3/10 | Meets minimum requirements with nothing distinguishing. Only consider if candidate pool is limited. |
| B+ | Meets with Highlights | 4/10 | Meets requirements with notable strengths in 1-2 dimensions. Shows potential but not a strong overall match. |
| A- | Good Match | 5/10 | Good overall fit with minor gaps. Meets most requirements with some areas of strength. |
| A | Strong Match | 6/10 | Strong alignment across most dimensions. Clearly qualified with demonstrated capability. |
| A+ | Excellent Match | 7/10 | Exceeds requirements in several dimensions. High-quality candidate with strong evidence of success. |
| S | Outstanding | 8/10 | Exceptional candidate. Exceeds requirements in most dimensions. Brings additional value beyond the JD. |
| SS | Top-Tier | 9/10 | Top-tier talent. Exceptional across nearly all dimensions. Would be a standout hire anywhere. |
| SSS | Perfect Match | 10/10 | Perfect alignment in every dimension. World-class qualifications. Once-in-a-career candidate. |

## Distribution Guidance

Maintain rigorous grading standards:

- **SSS**: < 1% of candidates. Reserve for truly extraordinary matches.
- **SS**: Top 2-3%. Genuinely exceptional, multi-dimensional excellence.
- **S**: Top 5-10%. Outstanding with clear differentiators.
- **A+ / A / A-**: Top 10-40%. The "clearly qualified" range.
- **B+ / B / B-**: 40-70%. "Potentially qualified but with concerns."
- **C**: Bottom 30%. Clear mismatches.

Resist grade inflation. When uncertain between two grades, lean toward the lower one and explain why.

## Hard Requirement Grade Cap Rule

Hard requirements (must-haves explicitly stated in JD) act as grade caps:

| Hard Req Status | Overall Grade Cap |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| All met | No cap |
| 1 minor hard req unmet | A (max) |
| 1 major hard req unmet | B+ (max) |
| 2+ hard reqs unmet | B (max) |
| Most hard reqs unmet | C |

Examples of hard requirements:
- Required degree level (e.g., "Must have Master's degree")
- Minimum years of experience (e.g., "5+ years required")
- Required certifications (e.g., "CPA required")
- Required language proficiency (e.g., "Fluent English required")
- Required specific skills (e.g., "Must know Kubernetes")

## Weighted Dimension Rules

Overall grade is NOT a simple average. Apply these rules:

1. **Weight by JD emphasis**: Dimensions emphasized in the JD carry 2-3x weight.
2. **Hard-req dimensions cap the grade** (see above).
3. **Soft-req dimensions boost**: Can raise grade by at most one level, but cannot compensate for hard requirement failures.
4. **Exceptional strength bonus**: If a candidate is SSS-level in a high-weight dimension, consider boosting overall by one level.
5. **Red flag penalty**: Unexplained career gaps, inconsistencies, or concerns in critical dimensions can lower the grade by one level.

## Grade Assignment Process

1. Score each of the 9 dimensions independently (C through SSS).
2. Identify which dimensions are hard-requirement vs. soft-requirement based on JD.
3. Check hard-requirement grade caps.
4. Calculate weighted assessment considering JD emphasis.
5. Apply exceptional strength bonus or red flag penalty if applicable.
6. Determine final overall grade with clear reasoning.
