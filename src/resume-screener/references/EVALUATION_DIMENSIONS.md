# Evaluation Dimensions

9 dimensions for comprehensive resume evaluation. For each dimension: find evidence in the resume, assess match quality against JD, assign a grade.

---

## 1. Education Background Match

**Measures**: Alignment between candidate's education and JD requirements.

**Evidence markers**:
- Degree level (PhD / Master's / Bachelor's / Associate / High School)
- Field of study relevance
- University ranking/reputation
- GPA / honors / Dean's list
- Relevant coursework or thesis topic
- Study abroad experience

**Grade indicators**:
- C: No degree or completely irrelevant field
- B: Has a degree but field is tangential
- A: Matches required degree level and field
- S: Top-tier university + highly relevant specialization
- SSS: Top institution + perfect field match + outstanding academic honors

**Weighting**: Higher for entry-level/junior roles. Lower for senior roles with 10+ years of experience where track record matters more.

**Bilingual notes**:
- CN: Recognize 985/211/C9/Double First-Class university tiers
- EN: Recognize Ivy League, Russell Group, QS/THE rankings, community college vs. 4-year distinction

---

## 2. Work Experience Relevance & Depth

**Measures**: How closely past roles match the target position in scope, domain, and seniority.

**Evidence markers**:
- Total years of relevant experience
- Role titles and seniority progression
- Company reputation and scale
- Industry relevance
- Responsibility descriptions
- Quantified achievements (revenue, users, team size, efficiency gains)

**Grade indicators**:
- C: No relevant work experience
- B: Tangentially related experience, different function or industry
- A: Directly relevant roles with clear responsibilities
- S: Deep expertise in exact domain + quantified high-impact achievements
- SSS: Industry-defining work at top-tier companies in the exact role

**Weighting**: Almost always high weight. This is typically the most important dimension.

**Bilingual notes**:
- CN: Recognize BAT (Baidu/Alibaba/Tencent), ByteDance, Meituan, JD.com, Huawei, state-owned enterprise tiers
- EN: Recognize FAANG/MAANG, Fortune 500, Big 4, unicorn startups

---

## 3. Technical/Professional Skills Match

**Measures**: Coverage and depth of required skills, tools, and technologies.

**Evidence markers**:
- Skill keywords matching JD requirements
- Technology versions and currency (e.g., Python 3.x vs 2.x)
- Depth indicators ("used" vs. "designed" vs. "architected")
- Breadth of complementary skills
- Tool/platform proficiency

**Grade indicators**:
- C: Missing most required skills
- B: Has some required skills, missing critical ones
- A: Covers all required skills with adequate depth
- S: Exceeds required skills + has advanced complementary skills
- SSS: Thought-leader level expertise across all required areas

**Weighting**: High for technical roles. Lower for management/leadership roles.

**Common pitfalls**:
- Don't equate "listing a skill" with "having deep expertise"
- Look for evidence of actual usage in projects/roles, not just keyword listing
- Consider skill currency — 5-year-old framework experience may be outdated

---

## 4. Project Experience Relevance

**Measures**: Relevance, complexity, and impact of project experience.

**Evidence markers**:
- Project scale (users, data volume, team size)
- Technology stack overlap with JD
- Problem domain similarity
- Quantified impact (performance improvement, cost reduction, user growth)
- Role in the project (contributor vs. lead vs. architect)
- Open source contributions

**Grade indicators**:
- C: No relevant project experience
- B: Small-scale or tangentially related projects
- A: Directly relevant projects with measurable impact
- S: Complex, high-impact projects in the exact domain
- SSS: Flagship/industry-recognized projects with transformative impact

**Weighting**: Higher for IC (individual contributor) roles. Critical for portfolio-based roles (design, engineering).

---

## 5. Leadership & Management Capability

**Measures**: Evidence of leadership, team management, and organizational influence.

**Evidence markers**:
- Team size managed
- "Led/managed/directed/mentored" action verbs
- Cross-functional coordination
- Budget/resource management
- Strategic decision-making evidence
- Mentoring or coaching references

**Grade indicators**:
- C: No leadership evidence
- B: Informal leadership (tech lead, project lead)
- A: Formal management with clear team and scope
- S: Multi-team or department-level leadership with strategic impact
- SSS: C-suite or organization-wide leadership with transformative results

**Weighting**: Critical for management/director roles. Low weight for junior IC roles. Medium for senior IC roles (tech lead expected).

---

## 6. Industry Domain Knowledge

**Measures**: Familiarity with the specific industry, regulations, and domain context.

**Evidence markers**:
- Industry-specific terminology usage
- Regulatory/compliance awareness
- Domain-specific certifications
- Industry conference participation or publications
- Prior companies in the same industry

**Grade indicators**:
- C: No industry exposure
- B: Adjacent industry experience
- A: Direct industry experience with domain understanding
- S: Deep domain expertise + regulatory knowledge
- SSS: Industry thought leader or subject matter expert

**Weighting**: Critical for regulated industries (finance, healthcare, legal). Lower for generalist tech roles.

---

## 7. Career Progression Trajectory

**Measures**: Growth pattern, ambition, and career momentum.

**Evidence markers**:
- Title progression speed
- Responsibility escalation over time
- Company tier progression (small → large, or reverse with reason)
- Lateral vs. upward moves
- Career gaps and their context
- Consistent tenure vs. frequent job-hopping

**Grade indicators**:
- C: Stagnant career, frequent lateral moves without growth, unexplained gaps
- B: Steady but slow progression
- A: Healthy upward trajectory matching career stage
- S: Accelerated progression with increasing scope
- SSS: Meteoric rise with exceptional responsibility growth

**Weighting**: Medium weight. Important signal for growth potential.

**Common pitfalls**:
- Short tenure is not always negative (startup environment, industry norm)
- Career gaps may have valid reasons (caregiving, education, health) — note as "insufficient data" rather than penalizing
- Industry switching can be a strength, not a weakness

---

## 8. Certifications & Achievements

**Measures**: Relevant professional certifications, awards, and recognitions.

**Evidence markers**:
- Professional certifications (PMP, CFA, AWS Certified, etc.)
- Industry awards
- Patents
- Publications (papers, articles, books)
- Open source contributions
- Speaking engagements / conference talks
- Competition rankings (ACM, Kaggle, etc.)

**Grade indicators**:
- C: No relevant certifications or achievements
- B: Basic/entry-level certifications
- A: Relevant professional certifications matching JD requirements
- S: Multiple high-value certifications + notable achievements
- SSS: Globally recognized awards, patents, or publications in the field

**Weighting**: Low for most tech roles unless JD specifically requires certifications. Higher for regulated professions (accounting, medicine, law).

---

## 9. Communication & Soft Skills Indicators

**Measures**: Communication quality inferred from resume presentation and content.

**Evidence markers**:
- Resume clarity, structure, and conciseness
- Action-verb usage and quantification
- Grammar and spelling accuracy
- Logical organization of information
- Language proficiency claims
- Cross-cultural or international experience
- Volunteer or community involvement

**Grade indicators**:
- C: Poorly written, disorganized, multiple errors
- B: Adequate but unremarkable presentation
- A: Well-structured, clear, professional quality
- S: Exceptionally well-crafted with strong narrative
- SSS: Publication-quality writing with compelling personal brand

**Weighting**: Medium. Higher for client-facing, writing-heavy, or leadership roles.

**Important note**: This is an indirect measure. Evaluate the resume as a communication artifact, but recognize that resume writing skill does not perfectly correlate with on-the-job communication ability.

**Bilingual bonus**: If the role requires bilingual ability and the resume demonstrates proficiency in both languages, boost this dimension.
